Remembering Jim Weirich

Back in May 2013, I had just learned Ruby and wanted to learns Rails with the Hartl Tutorial. I remember being up at 1:30 in the morning, frustrated why I had to type in rake db:migrate for the billionth time and wanted to better understand what was going on underneath the covers. I did what any good programmer would do and googled “Rake tutorials”, “How does rake work?”, and “What is Rake?”. I eventually stumbled across Jim Weirich’s “Basic Rake”.

“Its just Ruby code”, he said in his jovial manner. His voice was reassuring that everything would be ok. After finishing the video, I still had no idea what I was doing, but I did have a sense of hope that there was a person out there who I have never met who wanted to help me. It showed me that programming wasn’t a isolated experience, but a communal process, of people helping each other.

Even though I never met Jim, he and his talks have influenced me and how I program. Because he put people first, he taught that being a programmer wasn’t about knowing the latest Javascript framework or being a master of the newest database, but a programmer is great when he or she gives full heartedly to the community.

As I and my fellow students leave gSchool at the end of the week, we are going to get kicked out of the nest and enter the Ruby community. I hope that we all follow Jim’s example and give back through conference talks, mentorship, and open source.

Thank you Jim.
